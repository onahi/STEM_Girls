WHAT IS STEM?

STEM Stands for Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics.

  • SCIENCE
  • TECHNOLOGY
  • ENGINEERING
  • MATHEMATICS

WHY IS STEM IMPORTANT?

In the future the majority of jobs will increasingly require STEM skills. Yet considerably less gilrs than boys approach STEM.

Even though Women make half of the population, only 35% of STEM students in higher education globally are women, while the female representation in STEM careers is less than 30% (UNESCO)

Our Girls' education programs seeks to break barriers including ending child marriage, FGM, teenage pregnancy, rape, teacher-student sexual relationship, access to scholarship and advance girls' right to education.

STEM GIRLS INITIATIVE

STEM GIRLS Initiative empowers girls and advances gender equality in Girls Education particularly in the field of Science & Technology. It includes three parts:

  • STEM Girls School club
  • STEM Girls 2 STEM Women
  • STEM Girls Talkshow

STEM Girls school club

A study group for girls to improve girls'academic performance in school.

STEM Girls 2 STEM Women

A mentoring program for Stem Girls.

STEM Girls Talkshow

A weekly interactive session that brings together women working in the fields of STEM with Girls interested in STEM.

"The challenges are there - from home, school, culture practices and stereotyping, but young girls and women are beginning to break the barriers. "

- Prof Florence Onyemachi Nduka

STEM GIRLS is open to all Adolescent Girls and Young Women. join us, Get involved and be our STEM GIRLS, or help shape their future
